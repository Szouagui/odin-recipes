Moroccan couscous

Description

Moroccan couscous is a flavorful and aromatic dish that is traditionally made with steamed semolina grains and a variety of vegetables, meat, or fish. The dish is typically seasoned with a blend of spices including cumin, coriander, turmeric, and paprika, which gives it a distinctive flavor and fragrance. The vegetables used in the dish can vary depending on the region, but common additions include carrots, onions, chickpeas, and zucchini. Meat such as lamb or chicken is often added for extra protein and flavor. The couscous is usually served with a spicy tomato sauce on the side, which is used to moisten the grains and add extra flavor. Moroccan couscous is a filling and satisfying meal that is perfect for sharing with friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lamb or beef, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 zucchinis, chopped
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 4 cups chicken or beef broth
  • 1 cup couscous
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Heat some oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the lamb or beef and cook until browned on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the carrots, zucchini, and chickpeas to the pot and cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the cumin, coriander, turmeric, and paprika and cook for another minute.
  5. Return the lamb or beef to the pot and pour in the chicken or beef broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 45 minutes to an hour, until the meat is tender.
  6. While the meat is cooking, prepare the couscous according to package directions.
  7. When the meat is tender, season the stew with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. To serve, spoon some couscous into a bowl and ladle the stew on top. Sprinkle with raisins and toasted almonds.
